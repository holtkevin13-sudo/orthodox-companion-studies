Week 2 Priority Terms

These four terms are central to this week's study of Ignatius and Polycarp. Master these first, then explore the complete vocabulary below.

σάρξ
sarx
/sarks/
Flesh
The physical, material nature of humanity and of the incarnate Christ. In Ignatius's anti-Docetic theology, this term is crucial for affirming the reality of Christ's human nature against those who denied his true bodily existence.
Ignatian Usage
"He is truly of the race of David according to the flesh (κατὰ σάρκα), but Son of God by divine will and power" - Ignatius emphasizes Christ's genuine humanity against Docetic denials.
ignatius
ἕνωσις
henosis
/HEN-oh-sis/
Unity / Union
The fundamental unity that characterizes authentic Christianity - unity between God and humanity in Christ, unity within the Church under episcopal authority, and unity of faith and practice among believers.
Ignatian Usage
Ignatius sees unity (ἕνωσις) as the essential mark of the true Church, achieved through submission to the bishop as God's representative and through the Eucharist as the medicine of immortality.
ignatius
ἐπίσκοπος
episkopos
/eh-PIS-ko-pos/
Bishop / Overseer
The chief pastor and teacher of a local church, appointed by God to represent Christ's authority. Ignatius develops the theological foundation for the monarchical episcopate as God's will for Church order.
Ignatian Usage
"Follow the bishop (ἐπίσκοπος) as Jesus Christ follows the Father" - Ignatius establishes the theological principle that episcopal authority reflects divine hierarchy.
ignatius
μαρτυρία
martyria
/mar-too-REE-ah/
Witness / Martyrdom
The supreme testimony to Christ's truth, encompassing both verbal witness and the ultimate witness of death for the faith. Polycarp's martyrdom establishes the theological framework for understanding Christian suffering as witness.
Polycarp's Legacy
Polycarp's martyrdom transforms μαρτυρία from simple testimony into the supreme form of Christian witness, demonstrating that faithfulness unto death confirms the truth of the Gospel.
polycarp
Θεοφόρος
Theophoros
/theh-oh-FOR-os/
God-Bearer
Ignatius's distinctive self-designation, meaning "one who bears/carries God." This term expresses the intimate relationship between the Christian and the divine, suggesting that believers become vessels of God's presence in the world.
Ignatian Identity
"Ignatius, who is also called Theophoros (Θεοφόρος)" - This title becomes central to Ignatian spirituality, expressing both mystical union with God and apostolic authority.
ignatius
φάρμακον ἀθανασίας
pharmakon athanasias
/FAR-ma-kon ah-than-ah-SEE-as/
Medicine of Immortality
Ignatius's distinctive description of the Eucharist as the remedy against death and the guarantee of eternal life. This phrase establishes the soteriological significance of the Eucharist in early Christian theology.
Ignatian Sacramental Theology
The Eucharist is "the medicine of immortality (φάρμακον ἀθανασίας), the antidote against death, enabling us to live forever in Jesus Christ."
ignatius
παράδοσις
paradosis
/par-ah-DOH-sis/
Tradition / Transmission
The faithful transmission of apostolic teaching and practice from one generation to the next. Polycarp embodies this concept as a direct link between the Apostolic Age and the post-apostolic Church.
Apostolic Continuity
As a student of the Apostle John, Polycarp represents living tradition (παράδοσις), ensuring that apostolic teaching continues unchanged through episcopal succession.
polycarp
ὁμολογία
homologia
/hoh-moh-loh-GEE-ah/
Confession / Agreement
The public declaration of faith, especially under persecution. Polycarp's final confession before the Roman authorities becomes the paradigm for Christian faithfulness in the face of death.
Polycarp's Final Confession
Polycarp's confession (ὁμολογία) before the proconsul demonstrates that authentic faith must be publicly declared regardless of consequences: "Eighty-six years I have served Christ."
polycarp
πίστις
pistis
/PIS-tis/
Faith / Faithfulness
Both the act of believing and the quality of faithfulness over time. Polycarp's 86-year Christian life exemplifies πίστις as enduring commitment to Christ through every trial and circumstance.
Lifelong Faithfulness
Polycarp's faith (πίστις) spans nearly nine decades, demonstrating that authentic Christian commitment grows stronger with age and testing rather than weakening.
polycarp
ἐκκλησία
ekklesia
/eh-klee-SEE-ah/
Church / Assembly
The called-out assembly of believers united under episcopal authority. Ignatius develops the theological understanding of the Church as a hierarchical community centered on the bishop, presbyters, and deacons.
Ignatian Ecclesiology
The Church (ἐκκλησία) exists wherever the bishop is present, as Christ is present wherever the Father is. This establishes the episcopate as essential to Church identity.
ignatius
πρεσβύτερος
presbyteros
/pres-BOO-ter-os/
Elder / Presbyter
The second order of clergy in the Ignatian hierarchy, serving as counselors to the bishop. Ignatius establishes the presbyters as essential to church order, representing divine wisdom and apostolic council.
Hierarchical Order
The presbyters (πρεσβύτεροι) form the council of the bishop, just as the apostles formed the council of Christ. This creates the theological foundation for collegiate governance.
ignatius
διάκονος
diakonos
/dee-AH-ko-nos/
Deacon / Servant
The third order of clergy in the Ignatian system, representing service and ministry. Ignatius establishes deacons as essential to church order, embodying Christ's ministry of service to humanity.
Ministerial Service
The deacons (διάκονοι) represent the ministry of Jesus Christ, who came not to be served but to serve. They complete the threefold structure of church order.
ignatius
εὐχαριστία
eucharistia
/ev-kha-ris-TEE-ah/
Thanksgiving / Eucharist
The central sacramental act of Christian worship, understood by Ignatius as the source and summit of church unity. The Eucharist makes Christ truly present and binds the community together under episcopal authority.
Sacramental Unity
One Eucharist (εὐχαριστία), one cup, one altar, one bishop - Ignatius sees the Eucharist as the guarantee of church unity and the antidote to schism.
ignatius
ἀγάπη
agape
/ah-GAH-pay/
Divine Love
The self-sacrificial love that characterizes God's nature and should characterize Christian relationships. Both Ignatius and Polycarp demonstrate agape through their willingness to die for Christ and their pastoral care for their communities.
Martyrological Love
True love (ἀγάπη) is demonstrated not in words but in the willingness to lay down one's life. Both fathers show that martyrdom is the supreme expression of divine love.
ignatius polycarp
δόξα
doxa
/DOHK-sah/
Glory / Honor
The radiant manifestation of God's presence and majesty. Polycarp's martyrdom becomes a doxology, glorifying God through faithful witness and transforming death into worship and praise.
Martyrological Glory
Polycarp's final prayer transforms his execution into an act of glory (δόξα) to God, demonstrating that martyrdom is the supreme form of divine worship.
polycarp
ἀλήθεια
aletheia
/ah-LAY-thay-ah/
Truth
Ultimate reality as revealed in Christ and preserved in the apostolic tradition. Ignatius contrasts divine truth with false teaching, particularly Docetism, emphasizing that truth must be embodied, not merely proclaimed.
Embodied Truth
Truth (ἀλήθεια) is not merely doctrinal correctness but the reality of Christ's incarnation and the authenticity of Christian life lived in submission to divine authority.
ignatius
ψυχή
psyche
/psy-KHAY/
Soul / Life
The spiritual essence of human existence that survives bodily death. Polycarp's willingness to surrender his soul demonstrates that physical death cannot touch the true life that comes from God.
Eternal Perspective
Polycarp's calm acceptance of death shows his understanding that the soul (ψυχή) belongs to God and cannot be harmed by human persecution.
polycarp
κοινωνία
koinonia
/koy-no-NEE-ah/
Fellowship / Communion
The deep spiritual fellowship that unites Christians with God and with each other. Ignatius sees this communion as dependent on unity with the bishop and participation in the Eucharist.
Sacramental Fellowship
True fellowship (κοινωνία) exists only where there is one bishop, one Eucharist, and one faith. Division destroys communion with God and neighbor.
ignatius
ὑπομονή
hypomone
/hoo-po-mo-NAY/
Endurance / Patience
The steadfast endurance that enables Christians to remain faithful through trials and persecution. Polycarp's 86-year Christian life exemplifies this patient endurance that trusts in God's timing.
Faithful Endurance
Polycarp's endurance (ὑπομονή) through decades of Christian service and final martyrdom demonstrates the patient faithfulness that characterizes mature discipleship.
polycarp
βασιλεία
basileia
/bah-see-LAY-ah/
Kingdom / Reign
The sovereign rule of God established through Christ and extended through the Church. Polycarp's final words acknowledge Christ as his King, demonstrating that divine kingdom transcends earthly authority.
Divine Sovereignty
"How can I blaspheme my King who saved me?" - Polycarp's confession acknowledges Christ's kingdom (βασιλεία) as the ultimate authority that supersedes Roman power.
polycarp
